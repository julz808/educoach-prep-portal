### 3. WORD PROBLEMS (24 questions)

**Sub-Skill 3A: Time Calculations**
- **Level 1, Q1:** "A clock is 15 minutes fast. If it shows 3:30, what is the correct time?"
  - Options: A) 3:00, B) 3:15, C) 3:30, D) 3:45, E) 4:00
  - Answer: B (3:15)
  - Explanation: Subtract 15 minutes from displayed time
  - Real-world: Clock accuracy

- **Level 1, Q9:** "It takes 45 minutes to wash a car. How many cars can be washed in 3 hours?"
  - Options: A) 3, B) 4, C) 5, D) 6, E) 7
  - Answer: B (4)
  - Explanation: 180 minutes ÷ 45 minutes = 4 cars
  - Real-world: Time management

- **Level 2, Q9:** "A train leaves at 2:45 PM and arrives at 5:15 PM. How long was the journey?"
  - Options: A) 2 hours, B) 2.5 hours, C) 3 hours, D) 3.5 hours, E) 4 hours
  - Answer: B (2.5 hours or 2 hours 30 minutes)
  - Explanation: Calculate time difference
  - Real-world: Journey duration

**Sub-Skill 3B: Money/Shopping Problems**
- **Level 1, Q13:** "Sarah has $50. She spends $23 on a book and $12 on lunch. How much does she have left?"
  - Options: A) $10, B) $12, C) $15, D) $18, E) $20
  - Answer: C ($15)
  - Explanation: $50 - $23 - $12 = $15
  - Real-world: Budget management

- **Level 2, Q10:** "A shirt costs $45 after a 10% discount. What was the original price?"
  - Options: A) $49.50, B) $50, C) $54, D) $55, E) $60
  - Answer: B ($50)
  - Explanation: $45 = 90% of original, so original = $45 ÷ 0.9 = $50
  - Real-world: Discount calculations

**Sub-Skill 3C: Measurement Conversions**
- **Level 1, Q8:** "How many meters are in 3.5 kilometers?"
  - Options: A) 35, B) 350, C) 3,500, D) 35,000, E) 350,000
  - Answer: C (3,500)
  - Explanation: 3.5 km × 1,000 m/km = 3,500 m
  - Real-world: Metric conversions

- **Level 3, Q7:** "Convert 2.5 hours to minutes"
  - Options: A) 120, B) 130, C) 140, D) 150, E) 160
  - Answer: D (150)
  - Explanation: 2.5 × 60 = 150 minutes
  - Real-world: Time conversions

**Sub-Skill 3D: Ratio and Proportion**
- **Level 1, Q11:** "In a class of 30 students, the ratio of boys to girls is 2:3. How many boys are there?"
  - Options: A) 10, B) 12, C) 15, D) 18, E) 20
  - Answer: B (12)
  - Explanation: 2 parts boys out of 5 total parts = 12 boys
  - Real-world: Ratio problems

- **Level 2, Q11:** "A recipe for 4 people needs 200g flour. How much for 6 people?"
  - Options: A) 250g, B) 275g, C) 300g, D) 325g, E) 350g
  - Answer: C (300g)
  - Explanation: 200g × 6 ÷ 4 = 300g
  - Real-world: Recipe scaling

- **Level 3, Q8:** "If 5 workers can complete a job in 12 days, how many days would 3 workers take?"
  - Options: A) 15, B) 18, C) 20, D) 24, E) 30
  - Answer: C (20)
  - Explanation: Inverse proportion: 5 × 12 = 60 worker-days, 60 ÷ 3 = 20 days
  - Real-world: Work rate problems

**Sub-Skill 3E: Distribution Problems**
- **Level 1, Q12:** "24 chocolates are shared equally among 6 children. How many does each child get?"
  - Options: A) 2, B) 3, C) 4, D) 5, E) 6
  - Answer: C (4)
  - Explanation: 24 ÷ 6 = 4
  - Real-world: Equal sharing

- **Level 2, Q7:** "60 pillows need to be packed into boxes. Each box holds 8 pillows. How many boxes are needed?"
  - Options: A) 6, B) 7, C) 8, D) 9, E) 10
  - Answer: C (8)
  - Explanation: 60 ÷ 8 = 7.5, round up to 8 boxes
  - Real-world: Packing problems

**Sub-Skill 3F: Multi-Variable Problems**
- **Level 1, Q10:** "Tom has 3 times as many video games as Sarah. If Tom has 24 games, how many does Sarah have?"
  - Options: A) 6, B) 7, C) 8, D) 9, E) 10
  - Answer: C (8)
  - Explanation: 24 ÷ 3 = 8
  - Real-world: Comparative quantities

- **Level 2, Q8:** "An aeroplane travels at 600 km/h. How far does it travel in 2.5 hours?"
  - Options: A) 1,200 km, B) 1,350 km, C) 1,500 km, D) 1,650 km, E) 1,800 km
  - Answer: C (1,500 km)
  - Explanation: 600 × 2.5 = 1,500 km
  - Real-world: Distance/speed/time

- **Level 3, Q11:** "A pond has 120 fish. 30% are goldfish, and the rest are koi. How many koi are there?"
  - Options: A) 36, B) 60, C) 72, D) 84, E) 90
  - Answer: D (84)
  - Explanation: 70% of 120 = 0.7 × 120 = 84
  - Real-world: Percentage problems

**Sub-Skill 3G: Weight and Equivalence**
- **Level 2, Q13:** "If 4 apples weigh the same as 6 oranges, and 3 oranges weigh 300g, how much do 2 apples weigh?"
  - Options: A) 150g, B) 200g, C) 250g, D) 300g, E) 350g
  - Answer: D (300g)
  - Explanation: 3 oranges = 300g, so 6 oranges = 600g = 4 apples, so 1 apple = 150g, 2 apples = 300g
  - Real-world: Complex equivalence

**Sub-Skill 3H: Multi-Step Logic**
- **Level 3, Q10:** "A school has 12 classes. Each class has 25 students. If 60 students are absent, how many are present?"
  - Options: A) 240, B) 250, C) 260, D) 270, E) 280
  - Answer: A (240)
  - Explanation: Total = 12 × 25 = 300, Present = 300 - 60 = 240
  - Real-world: Multi-step calculation

- **Level 3, Q12:** "A container holds 2.4 liters. How many 150ml cups can be filled?"
  - Options: A) 12, B) 14, C) 16, D) 18, E) 20
  - Answer: C (16)
  - Explanation: 2,400ml ÷ 150ml = 16 cups
  - Real-world: Volume conversion and division

- **Level 3, Q20:** "Two single digit numbers when multiplied together make 45. If the same two numbers are added to 10, what would be the total?"
  - Options: A) 55, B) 35, C) 14, D) 24, E) 42
  - Answer: D (24)
  - Explanation: 5 × 9 = 45, then 5 + 9 + 10 = 24
  - Real-world: Factor identification and arithmetic

- **Level 3, Q17:** "The average of five numbers is 20. Four of the numbers are 15, 18, 22, and 25. What is the fifth number?"

## Level Progression Analysis

### Level 1 (Year 7) - Foundational Skills
**Characteristics:**
- Simple arithmetic progressions (+6, ×2, ÷2)
- Basic word problems with single-step solutions
- Introduction to matrices with simple patterns
- Fundamental number properties (square roots, fractions)
- Real-world contexts: time, money, measurement
- Answer options widely spaced
- Clear, unambiguous wording

**Key Topics:**
- Time calculations (fast/slow clocks)
- Basic sequences
- Simple ratio and proportion
- Measurement conversions (km to m)
- Equal distribution
- GCF and LCM
- Basic fractions and decimals

### Level 2 (Year 8) - Intermediate Development
**Characteristics:**
- More complex patterns (Fibonacci, alternating sequences)
- Two-step word problems
- Matrices with multiplication/division rules
- Introduction to percentage problems
- More sophisticated real-world contexts
- Smaller gaps between answer options
- Multiple valid approaches required

**Key Topics:**
- Geometric progressions
- Recipe scaling (proportion)
- Packing problems (division with rounding)
- Distance/speed/time calculations
- Weight equivalence
- Complex ratio problems
- Percentage discounts

### Level 3 (Year 9) - Advanced Reasoning
**Characteristics:**
- Exponential and power sequences
- Multi-variable word problems (3+ steps)
- Complex matrix patterns
- Advanced proportion and inverse proportion
- Abstract reasoning required
- Very close answer options (requiring precise calculation)
- Multiple constraints in problems

**Key Topics:**
- Powers of 4, exponential growth
- Decimal division patterns
- Work rate problems (inverse proportion)
- Multi-step percentage calculations
- Factor identification combined with arithmetic
- Finding missing values from averages
- Prime number identification
- Complex volume conversions

### Skill Progression Summary

| Skill Area | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |
|------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Arithmetic** | Single operation | Two operations | Multi-step chains |
| **Patterns** | Simple +/× | Alternating, Fibonacci | Exponential, powers |
| **Word Problems** | Direct application | Two-step reasoning | Multi-variable logic |
| **Number Sense** | Whole numbers focus | Decimals introduced | Fractions/decimals fluent |
| **Abstraction** | Concrete contexts | Semi-abstract | Highly abstract |

- Avoid repetitive patterns in consecutive questions
