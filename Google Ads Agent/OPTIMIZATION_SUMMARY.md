## 🏗️ Ad Group Structure (Match Type Split)

Each campaign now has **3-4 ad groups** organized by match type:

### Structure Pattern:
```
Campaign
├── Ad group 1 (PAUSED) - Original mixed ad group
│   └── Contains all keywords (paused, kept for reference)
│
├── Ad group 1 | EXACT (ENABLED)
│   ├── EXACT match keywords only
│   └── 1 responsive search ad
│
├── Ad group 1 | PHRASE (ENABLED)
│   ├── PHRASE match keywords only
│   └── 1 responsive search ad
│
└── Ad group 1 | BROAD (ENABLED)
    ├── BROAD match keywords only
    └── 1 responsive search ad
```

### Why This Structure?

**Benefits of Match Type Segmentation:**
1. **Better control** - Different bid strategies per match type
2. **Easier optimization** - Identify which match types perform best
3. **Budget efficiency** - Prevent broad match from consuming entire budget
4. **Quality Score** - More relevant ad copy per match type
5. **Performance tracking** - Clear attribution to match type

## 🎯 Match Type Strategy Explained

### What Are the Three Ad Group Types?

Each campaign now has 3 separate ad groups for better control:

#### 1. **EXACT Match Ad Group** (`Ad group 1 | EXACT`)
**What it does:** Shows ads ONLY when someone searches the exact keyword

**Example:** Keyword "educourse"
- ✅ Shows for: "educourse"
- ❌ Doesn't show for: "educourse reviews", "educourse login", "what is educourse"

**Bid Strategy:** Highest bids (inherited from original keywords, e.g., $59.50 for "edutest practice tests")

**Best for:**
- High-intent keywords
- Branded searches ("educourse", "edutest practice tests")
- Known converting terms

**Campaigns using EXACT:**
- All 6 campaigns have EXACT match ad groups

---

#### 2. **PHRASE Match Ad Group** (`Ad group 1 | PHRASE`)
**What it does:** Shows ads when search includes the keyword phrase (can have words before/after)

**Example:** Keyword "year 7 naplan test"
- ✅ Shows for: "year 7 naplan test", "best year 7 naplan test", "year 7 naplan test free"
- ❌ Doesn't show for: "naplan test year 7" (word order matters)

**Bid Strategy:** Low bids ($0.01) to let EXACT get priority, scale up based on performance

**Best for:**
- Medium-intent searches
- Informational queries that might convert
- Testing new keyword variations

**Campaigns using PHRASE:**
- ACER: 10 phrase keywords (e.g., "Acer scholarship exam", "Acer test preparation")
- EduTest: 10 phrase keywords (all enabled in original, none moved - needs investigation)
- NSW: 10 phrase keywords (e.g., "James Ruse test prep", "North Sydney Girls test")
- Year 5 NAPLAN: 7 phrase keywords (e.g., "year 5 naplan preparation")
- Year 7 NAPLAN: 7 phrase keywords (e.g., "year 7 naplan test practice")
- VIC: 0 phrase keywords (only using EXACT + BROAD)

---

#### 3. **BROAD Match Ad Group** (`Ad group 1 | BROAD`)
**What it does:** Shows ads for searches related to the keyword (loosest matching)

**Example:** Keyword "vic selective test"
- ✅ Shows for: "vic selective test", "selective school victoria", "melbourne high school entrance exam"
- ⚠️ Might show for: "selective school nsw" (if Google thinks it's relevant)

**Bid Strategy:** Very low bids ($0.01) to capture volume without overspending

**Best for:**
- Discovering new search terms (what are people actually searching?)
- Capturing long-tail variations
- Budget-efficient awareness

**Campaigns using BROAD:**
- VIC: 26 broad keywords (most aggressive broad match usage)
- ACER: 1 broad keyword ("Acer practice papers")
- EduTest: 1 broad keyword ("edutest test papers")
- NSW: 1 broad keyword ("NSW selective entry practice test")
- Year 5 NAPLAN: 0 broad keywords
- Year 7 NAPLAN: 0 broad keywords

---

### Why Split by Match Type?

**Before (Mixed Ad Group):**
```
Ad group 1
├── "educourse" [EXACT] @ $6.71
├── "edutest practice test" [PHRASE] @ Auto bid
└── "edutest test papers" [BROAD] @ Auto bid
```

**Problem:**
- Broad match can consume entire budget
- Can't tell which match type performs best
- Hard to optimize (one bid for all?)
- Lower Quality Scores (generic ad copy for all)

**After (Split Ad Groups):**
```
Ad group 1 | EXACT
└── "educourse" [EXACT] @ $6.71
    → High bid, tight control, branded traffic

Ad group 1 | PHRASE
└── "edutest practice test" [PHRASE] @ $0.01
    → Low bid, testing variations

Ad group 1 | BROAD
└── "edutest test papers" [BROAD] @ $0.01
    → Low bid, discovery mode
```

**Benefits:**
- **Budget control:** EXACT gets most budget (high bids), PHRASE/BROAD get limited budget (low bids)
- **Performance tracking:** Easy to see "EXACT converts at $50 CAC, PHRASE at $80, BROAD at $120"
- **Optimization:** Can pause entire match type if not performing
- **Quality Score:** Separate ad copy optimized for match type specificity
- **Scaling:** Can increase PHRASE/BROAD bids if they perform well

## 🎓 Glossary

**EXACT Match:** Shows ad only for exact keyword (e.g., "educourse")
**PHRASE Match:** Shows ad when search includes keyword phrase (e.g., "best educourse review")
**BROAD Match:** Shows ad for related searches (e.g., "online course platforms")

**RSA (Responsive Search Ad):** Google's ad format with multiple headlines/descriptions that auto-optimizes
**Quality Score:** Google's 1-10 rating of keyword relevance (affects ad rank and CPC)
**Impression Share:** % of possible impressions your ads received
**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Cost per conversion

**Match Type Split:** Separating keywords into different ad groups by match type (EXACT/PHRASE/BROAD)
**Negative Keyword:** Keyword that prevents your ad from showing (e.g., "free" blocks freeloaders)
