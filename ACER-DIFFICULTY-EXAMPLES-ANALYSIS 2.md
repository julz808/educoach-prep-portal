## üìä DIFFICULTY PROGRESSION PATTERNS

### What Makes Questions EASY (Difficulty 1):
1. ‚úÖ Information is **explicitly stated** in passage
2. ‚úÖ **One-step reasoning:** Find ‚Üí Match ‚Üí Answer
3. ‚úÖ Answer is in **one location** in text
4. ‚úÖ Clear, **unambiguous** correct answer
5. ‚úÖ Distractors are obviously wrong

**Examples:**
- "In the passage, the word 'remote' means:" ‚Üí Simple definition lookup
- "What is the main purpose?" ‚Üí Stated in title/opening

### What Makes Questions MEDIUM (Difficulty 2):
1. ‚úÖ Requires **synthesizing** information from multiple places
2. ‚úÖ **Two-step reasoning:** Find A ‚Üí Find B ‚Üí Compare/Combine ‚Üí Answer
3. ‚úÖ Answer requires **understanding relationships** (contrast, cause-effect)
4. ‚úÖ Must distinguish between **details vs. main ideas**
5. ‚úÖ Distractors are plausible but incomplete

**Examples:**
- "The passage contrasts X with Y. What is the key difference?" ‚Üí Find X + Find Y + Compare
- "What is the central theme?" ‚Üí Combine multiple paragraphs into one theme

### What Would Make Questions HARD (Difficulty 3):
1. ‚ùì Would require **deep inference** not stated in text
2. ‚ùì **Multi-step reasoning:** 3+ logical steps
3. ‚ùì Answer requires understanding **implicit relationships**
4. ‚ùì Must evaluate **author's purpose, tone, or unstated assumptions**
5. ‚ùì Distractors are subtle and require careful reasoning to eliminate

**Examples (NOT FOUND in your database):**

- "How does the author's use of Y in paragraph 2 support the implicit argument in paragraph 5?"
