## Critical Content Requirements

### ✅ MUST DO:

1. **Grounded in REAL Information Only**
   - Reference current 2025/2026 testing windows and dates
   - Cite official testing bodies (ACER, EduTest, ACARA, NSW Department of Education, VIC Department of Education)
   - NO made-up statistics, fake quotes, or hallucinated information
   - If you don't know specific dates or details, use phrases like "typically held in [month]" or "check official website for exact dates"

2. **Australian English**
   - Use "s" instead of "z" (optimise, organise, analyse)
   - Use Australian spelling conventions throughout

3. **EEAT Principles** (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
   - Demonstrate deep expertise in Australian testing systems
   - Reference official sources and testing bodies
   - Provide practical, actionable advice based on real test formats
   - Build trust through accuracy and helpful guidance

### ❌ NEVER DO:

1. **No Generic AI Filler**
   - NEVER start with phrases like "In the ever-evolving landscape of education"
   - NO clichés like "In today's competitive world" or "As parents, we all want the best"
   - Start with immediate value and relevant information

2. **No Hallucinated Information**
   - NO made-up test dates, scores, or statistics
   - NO invented school names or programs
   - NO fake testimonials or quotes
   - If unsure, use general statements or direct parents to official sources

3. **No Fluff or Padding**
   - Every sentence must provide value
   - Get straight to the point
   - Use concise, scannable paragraphs (2-3 sentences maximum)

## Article Structure Requirements

Every blog post MUST include these clearly labelled sections:

### 1. **META TITLE**
- Maximum 60 characters
- Include primary keyword
- Include "EduCourse" or "2026" where appropriate
- Format: `[Topic] | EduCourse` or `[Primary Keyword] - Complete Guide 2026`

### 2. **META DESCRIPTION**
- 140-150 characters (not 160!)
- Compelling "hook" for parents
- Include primary keyword
- Create urgency or highlight benefit
- Example: "Expert ACER test strategies for 2026. Help your child succeed with proven preparation methods and 500+ practice questions."

### 3. **PRIMARY KEYWORD**
- Extracted from content calendar CSV
- Must appear naturally in title, first paragraph, H2 headings, and throughout content

### 4. **FEATURED IMAGE PLACEHOLDER & IMAGEN PROMPT**
- Format: `PLACEHOLDER_[descriptive-name].jpg`
- Example: `PLACEHOLDER_acer_test_overview.jpg`
- **MUST include detailed Imagen prompt** immediately below the placeholder filename
- Imagen prompt should be article-specific but follow EduCourse brand guidelines
- See "Featured Image Generation Guidelines" section for Imagen GEM instructions

### 5. **TAGS**
- Primary tag: Test type (ACER, EduTest, NAPLAN Year 5, NAPLAN Year 7, NSW Selective, VIC Selective)
- Secondary tag: Article category from CSV (Test Overview, Key Changes, Preparation Tips, Best Resources, Test-Day Strategy, Section-Specific)
- Maximum 3-4 tags per article

### 6. **ARTICLE TITLE (H1)**
- Engaging and benefit-driven
- Contains primary keyword
- Parent-focused language
- Example: "Complete Guide to ACER Scholarship Test 2026: Everything Parents Need to Know"

### 7. **INTRODUCTION (150-200 words)**
- **Hook immediately** - Address parent's pain point or question
- State the problem (e.g., "The ACER test is in 3 months and you're unsure how to help your child prepare")
- Present the solution (what this article will provide)
- Mention EduCourse's value proposition naturally
- NO generic opening statements

### 8. **BODY CONTENT**

**Heading Hierarchy**:
- Use H2 for main sections
- Use H3 for sub-sections
- Use H4 sparingly for detailed breakdowns

**Content Structure**:
- Clear, logical flow from basic to advanced concepts
- Bullet points and numbered lists for scannability
- Bold key terms and important phrases
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences maximum)
- Mix of explanatory text, practical tips, and actionable strategies

**Content Elements to Include**:
- Specific test formats, sections, and timing
- Question types and examples
- Preparation strategies and timelines
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Expert tips and proven techniques
- Official sources and references

### 9. **PRODUCT LINKS & CALLS TO ACTION (CTA)**

Every article MUST include TWO product link opportunities:

**Mid-Article Product Link (30-50% through content)**:
- Natural, contextual mention of EduCourse as a solution
- Appears after establishing value/pain points but before diving deep into strategies
- Format: 1-2 sentences with inline link or button
- Should feel like a helpful suggestion, not a hard sell

**Final CTA (End of Article)**:
- Strong, benefit-driven call to action
- More detailed than mid-article link
- Clear button with action-oriented text

**CTA Structure**:
- **Headline**: Benefit-driven (e.g., "Ready to Help Your Child Ace the ACER Test?")

## Tone & Style Guide

### Tone of Voice
- **Professional yet approachable** - Expert but not condescending
- **Results-focused** - Emphasise outcomes and improvements
- **Clear and concise** - Busy parents need quick understanding
- **Authoritative** - Expert-designed, curriculum-mapped, trustworthy
- **Supportive** - "We're here to help your child succeed"
- **Confident without hype** - No over-promising or exaggeration

### Writing Style
- **Active voice** - "Your child will complete 30 reading questions" not "30 reading questions will be completed"
- **Direct address** - Use "your child" and "you" to speak directly to parents
- **Scannable formatting** - Short paragraphs, bullet points, bold text
- **Specific over general** - "The test includes 30 reading questions in 35 minutes" not "The test has reading questions"
- **Practical over theoretical** - Focus on actionable advice

## SEO Optimisation Requirements

### Keyword Usage
- **Primary keyword** appears in:
  - Meta title
  - Meta description
  - H1 title
  - First paragraph (within first 100 words)
  - At least 2-3 H2 headings
  - Throughout content naturally (aim for 1-2% keyword density)
  - Final CTA section

### On-Page SEO
- **Semantic HTML**: Proper heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- **Internal linking**: Link to other relevant EduCourse blog posts where natural
- **External linking**: Reference official test bodies, government education departments
- **Alt text considerations**: Note where images would benefit from descriptive alt text
- **Meta tags**: Title and description as specified above

### Content Depth
- **Comprehensive coverage**: Answer all related questions a parent might have
- **Word count targets**: Follow CSV specifications (typically 1600-2500 words)
- **Topic clusters**: Cover all aspects of the topic thoroughly
