ARTICLE TITLE:
Year 5 NAPLAN Numeracy 2026: Complete Strategy Guide

INTRODUCTION:

The Year 5 NAPLAN Numeracy test assesses mathematical understanding and problem-solving skills developed throughout primary school. Students face 50 minutes to answer approximately 50 questions covering Number and Algebra, Measurement and Geometry, and Statistics and Probability. The test is divided into two sections: a non-calculator section testing mental computation and number sense, followed by a calculator section with an on-screen calculator for more complex problems.

Success requires solid understanding of Year 5 mathematics concepts, efficient problem-solving strategies, ability to interpret questions correctly (many are word problems), and effective time management across both calculator and non-calculator sections.

This comprehensive guide explains what the Numeracy test assesses, breaks down both test sections, provides problem-solving strategies, and outlines effective practice approaches aligned with the Australian Curriculum.

H2: Understanding Year 5 NAPLAN Numeracy

**Test Format:**
- **Time:** 50 minutes total
- **Questions:** Approximately 50 questions
- **Two Sections:**
  - Non-calculator section (approximately 20-25 questions)
  - Calculator section (approximately 25-30 questions)
- **Format:** Multiple choice, drag-and-drop, numeric entry
- **Delivery:** NAPLAN Online platform with on-screen calculator in second section

**What's Assessed:**
- Number and place value
- Addition, subtraction, multiplication, division
- Fractions and decimals
- Patterns and algebra
- Measurement (length, area, volume, time, money)
- Shape and geometry
- Data and probability


H2: Non-Calculator Section

Testing mental math and number sense.

**H3: What the Non-Calculator Section Tests**

**Focus Areas:**
- Mental computation
- Number sense and estimation
- Basic operations
- Understanding of place value
- Simple fractions and decimals
- Number patterns

**Why No Calculator:**
Tests whether students have strong foundational number sense and can perform calculations mentally or with simple written methods.

**Time Allocation:**
- Approximately 20-25 minutes for non-calculator section
- Questions generally quicker than calculator section
- About 1 minute per question

**H3: Non-Calculator Question Types**

**Basic Operations:**
- 347 + 289 = ?
- 56 × 8 = ?
- 420 ÷ 6 = ?

**Strategy:** Use mental math strategies (breaking apart, rounding and adjusting, known facts)

**Place Value:**
- What is the value of the 7 in 47,352?
- Which number is 1,000 more than 23,456?

**Strategy:** Understand that each place is 10 times the place to its right

**Number Patterns:**
- What number comes next: 3, 7, 11, 15, ___?
- Complete the pattern: 2, 4, 8, 16, ___?

**Strategy:** Look for what's changing (adding, multiplying, alternating)

**Simple Fractions:**
- What is 1/2 of 24?
- Which fraction is equivalent to 2/4?

**Strategy:** Use fraction models mentally, simplify fractions

**H3: Mental Math Strategies**

**Addition/Subtraction:**
- **Break apart:** 47 + 38 = 47 + 40 - 2 = 85
- **Compensation:** 99 + 45 = 100 + 44 = 144
- **Make tens:** 17 + 8 = 17 + 3 + 5 = 25

**Multiplication:**
- **Known facts:** 8 × 7 = 56 (memorized)
- **Double and double:** 4 × 15 = 2 × 30 = 60
- **Distributive:** 6 × 13 = (6 × 10) + (6 × 3) = 60 + 18 = 78

**Division:**
- **Known facts:** 42 ÷ 6 = 7
- **Think multiplication:** 56 ÷ 8 = ? (What times 8 equals 56?)
- **Halving:** 64 ÷ 4 = 32 ÷ 2 = 16

**Estimation:**
- Round to friendly numbers
- Check if answer is reasonable
- Use for answer elimination


H2: Calculator Section

More complex problems with tool support.

**H3: What the Calculator Section Tests**

**Focus Areas:**
- Multi-step problems
- More complex calculations
- Word problems requiring interpretation
- Data analysis
- Geometry and measurement
- Real-world applications

**Calculator Available:**
On-screen calculator provided (students must click to use)

**Time Allocation:**
- Approximately 25-30 minutes for calculator section
- Questions generally more complex than non-calculator
- About 1 minute per question

**H3: Using the Calculator Effectively**

**When to Use Calculator:**
✓ Large or complex calculations
✓ Multi-step problems with interim calculations
✓ Decimal calculations
✓ Checking your mental math answer

**When NOT to Use Calculator:**
✗ Simple mental math (faster without)
✗ Questions about concepts (calculator won't help understanding)
✗ Every single calculation (wastes time)

**Calculator Tips:**
- Practice with on-screen calculator before test
- Clear calculator between problems
- Check that you entered numbers correctly
- Still estimate to verify answer is reasonable

**H3: Calculator Section Question Types**

**Multi-Step Word Problems:**
"Sarah bought 3 books at $12.95 each and 2 pens at $3.50 each. How much did she spend in total?"

**Strategy:**
1. Identify what you're solving for (total cost)
2. Break into steps (book cost, pen cost, add together)
3. Calculate: (3 × 12.95) + (2 × 3.50) = 38.85 + 7.00 = 45.85

**Data Interpretation:**
Given a table or graph, answer questions about the data.

**Strategy:**
- Read labels and titles carefully
- Identify what data shows
- Find specific information requested
- Perform calculations if needed

**Geometry and Measurement:**
- Calculate area, perimeter, volume
- Angle problems
- Scale and proportion

**Strategy:**
- Draw or visualize if helpful
- Remember formulas (area = length × width, etc.)
- Check units (cm, m, km)

H2: Year 5 Curriculum Content

What students should know by Year 5.

**H3: Number and Place Value**

**Whole Numbers:**
- Read, write, order numbers to at least 100,000
- Understand place value to hundred thousands
- Round numbers to nearest 10, 100, 1,000
- Add and subtract large numbers
- Multiply and divide by single and double-digit numbers

**Operations:**
- Know multiplication facts to 10 × 10
- Use efficient strategies for mental computation
- Solve problems using all four operations

**H3: Fractions and Decimals**

**Fractions:**
- Compare and order unit fractions (1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5)
- Understand equivalent fractions (1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6)
- Add and subtract fractions with same denominator
- Find fractions of quantities (1/3 of 12)

**Decimals:**
- Understand place value to hundredths
- Compare and order decimals to two decimal places
- Connect fractions and decimals (1/2 = 0.5, 1/4 = 0.25)

**H3: Measurement and Geometry**

**Measurement:**
- Choose appropriate units (mm, cm, m, km)
- Calculate perimeter and area of rectangles
- Read analog and digital time
- Convert between common units (cm to m, g to kg)
- Calculate elapsed time
- Solve money problems

**Geometry:**
- Classify angles (right, acute, obtuse, straight)
- Identify and describe properties of 2D shapes
- Identify and describe properties of 3D objects
- Symmetry and transformations

**H3: Statistics and Probability**

**Data:**
- Read and interpret tables, graphs, charts
- Create simple graphs
- Calculate mean (average)
- Identify mode (most common)

**Probability:**
- Describe likelihood (impossible, unlikely, likely, certain)
- Compare probabilities of simple events


H2: Problem-Solving Strategies

Approaches for tackling unfamiliar questions.

**H3: Universal Problem-Solving Process**

**Step 1: Understand the Question**
- Read carefully (twice if needed)
- What is being asked?
- What information is provided?
- What information do I need to find?

**Step 2: Plan Your Approach**
- What operation(s) do I need?
- Do I need multiple steps?
- Can I draw a picture or diagram?
- Can I estimate the answer?

**Step 3: Solve**
- Carry out your plan
- Show working (helps catch errors)
- Use calculator if needed
- Check interim results

**Step 4: Check**
- Is the answer reasonable?
- Does it make sense in context?
- Did I answer the question asked?

**H3: Specific Strategies**

**Draw a Picture or Diagram:**
Helpful for:
- Geometry problems
- Word problems with visual elements
- Fraction problems
- Patterns

**Make a Table or List:**
Helpful for:
- Organizing information
- Finding patterns
- Systematic problem-solving

**Work Backwards:**
Helpful for:
- Problems giving end result, asking for start
- Multi-step problems in reverse

**Guess and Check (Strategically):**
Helpful for:
- When calculation method isn't obvious
- Multiple choice (eliminate and test)

**Look for Patterns:**
Helpful for:
- Number sequences
- Algebra problems
- Geometry patterns


H2: Common Question Types and Approaches

**H3: Word Problems**

**Key Strategy:** Translate words into math.

**Common Keywords:**
- **Addition:** total, sum, altogether, combined, increase
- **Subtraction:** difference, less than, fewer, remaining, decrease
- **Multiplication:** times, product, of (as in "1/2 of 10"), each, per
- **Division:** split, share, divide, quotient, per (sometimes)

**Approach:**
1. Identify what you're solving for
2. Underline key information
3. Eliminate unnecessary information
4. Choose operation(s)
5. Calculate and check

**H3: Measurement Problems**

**Common Types:**
- Perimeter: Add all sides
- Area: Length × width (rectangles)
- Volume: Length × width × height
- Time: Read clocks, calculate elapsed time
- Money: Add, subtract costs, calculate change

**Key Skills:**
- Know formulas
- Pay attention to units
- Convert units when necessary (100 cm = 1 m)
- Draw diagram if helpful

**H3: Data Questions**

**Approach:**
- Read title of graph/table
- Understand what data represents
- Find specific data points requested
- Perform calculations if needed (totals, averages, differences)

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Misreading axes or labels
- Not noticing scale (each line = 2, not 1)
- Confusing rows and columns

**H3: Fraction and Decimal Problems**

**Visual Strategies:**
- Picture fraction models (circles, rectangles divided)
- Use number lines for comparing
- Remember equivalents (1/2 = 0.5, 1/4 = 0.25, 3/4 = 0.75)

**Operations:**
- Adding fractions: same denominator needed
- Finding fraction of amount: multiply
- Converting: fraction bar = division (3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75)


H2: Time Management

Managing 50 minutes across ~50 questions.

**H3: Suggested Time Allocation**

**Non-Calculator Section (20-25 questions):**
- 20-25 minutes
- Approximately 1 minute per question
- Move quickly (mental math should be fairly quick)

**Calculator Section (25-30 questions):**
- 25-28 minutes
- Approximately 1 minute per question
- Some questions faster, some slower

**Review:**
- 2-3 minutes at end
- Check flagged questions
- Verify you answered all questions

**H3: Pacing Strategy**

**Answer All Questions:**
- No penalty for wrong answers
- Never leave blank - always guess if unsure

**Don't Get Stuck:**
- If question taking too long (over 90 seconds), make best guess
- Flag question if possible
- Return if time permits

**Easy Questions First:**
- If question seems very difficult, skip and return
- Build confidence with questions you know
- Don't let one hard question derail entire test


H2: Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Calculation Errors:**
- Simple arithmetic mistakes
- Entering wrong numbers in calculator
- Forgetting to complete multi-step problems

**Prevention:** Check work, estimate answers, verify calculator entry

**Misreading Questions:**
- Not reading carefully
- Missing key words like "NOT" or "EXCEPT"
- Confusing what's being asked

**Prevention:** Read twice, underline key information

**Unit Errors:**
- Mixing units (adding cm and m without converting)
- Answer in wrong unit
- Not noticing unit requested in question

**Prevention:** Pay attention to units throughout problem

**Calculator Over-Reliance:**
- Using calculator for simple mental math
- Trusting calculator without checking reasonableness
- Wasting time on calculator for easy calculations

**Prevention:** Use calculator strategically, always estimate

H2: Supporting At-Home Practice

How parents can help.

**H3: Real-World Math**

**Daily Opportunities:**
- Shopping: Calculate totals, change, unit prices
- Cooking: Measurement, fractions (1/2 cup, 3/4 teaspoon)
- Time: Reading clocks, calculating duration
- Travel: Distance, time, speed concepts

**Value:** Builds number sense and real-world application

**H3: Mental Math Games**

**Quick Activities:**
- Math facts practice (flashcards, apps)
- Estimation games (how many in jar, distance to location)
- Number puzzles and logic games
- Online math games (in moderation)

**H3: Homework Support**

**Effective Support:**
- Help child understand problems, don't solve for them
- Ask questions that guide thinking
- Encourage showing working
- Celebrate effort and improvement
