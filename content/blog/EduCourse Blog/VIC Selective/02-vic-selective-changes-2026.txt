VIC Selective Entry 2026: What's Changed This Year

INTRODUCTION:

Victoria's Selective Entry High School test is one of the most competitive academic assessments in Australia, with approximately 5,000+ students competing annually for roughly 1,600 Year 9 places. Understanding exactly what has changed for 2026—and what remains the same—ensures your preparation focuses on the right priorities.

This comprehensive guide analyzes all confirmed changes to the 2026 VIC Selective Entry test including format updates, timing adjustments, section modifications, scoring changes, and application process updates. Whether you're starting preparation or adjusting an existing study plan, understanding these changes helps you prepare strategically and avoid outdated information.

H2: Summary of Key Changes for 2026

Understanding the overall change landscape helps contextualize specific updates.

H3: Major Confirmed Changes

**Test Format:**
The 2026 VIC Selective Entry test maintains its established five-section structure with no major format changes:
- General Ability - Verbal (60 questions, 30 minutes)
- General Ability - Quantitative (50 questions, 30 minutes)
- Mathematics Reasoning (60 questions, 30 minutes)
- Reading Reasoning (50 questions, 35 minutes)
- Writing (2 prompts, choose 1, 40 minutes)

**What This Means:**
Preparation strategies from recent years remain valid. The test continues to assess cognitive abilities and curriculum knowledge through the same proven structure.

H3: Minor Refinements for 2026

**Enhanced Online Platform:**
The Victorian Curriculum and Assessment Authority (VCAA) has made minor refinements to the online testing platform including improved navigation, clearer instructions, and updated accessibility features for students with special needs.

**Updated Practice Materials:**
Official practice materials released for 2026 reflect current question styles and difficulty calibration, providing families with the most accurate preparation resources.

**Scoring Transparency:**
While the "superior ability" threshold methodology remains unchanged, VCAA has provided clearer documentation about how section scores combine to determine eligibility for offers.


H2: Section-by-Section Analysis

Understanding what has and hasn't changed in each section clarifies preparation priorities.

H3: General Ability - Verbal (No Changes)

**Format:**
- 60 questions, 30 minutes (unchanged)
- Multiple choice with 5 options (unchanged)
- Question types: analogies, vocabulary, letter patterns, code breaking, logical deduction (unchanged)

**Content:**
Question difficulty and content remain consistent with previous years. The section continues to test cognitive reasoning with language rather than curriculum knowledge.

**Preparation Impact:**
Practice materials from 2024-2025 remain highly relevant. Focus continues on vocabulary development, pattern recognition, and rapid logical reasoning.

H3: General Ability - Quantitative (No Changes)

**Format:**
- 50 questions, 30 minutes (unchanged)
- No calculator permitted (unchanged)
- Question types: number series, number grids, pattern recognition, applied word problems (unchanged)

**Content:**
Question styles and difficulty calibration remain consistent. The section continues to emphasize pattern recognition and logical reasoning with numbers rather than curriculum mathematics.

**Preparation Impact:**
Existing preparation strategies remain valid. Continue focusing on rapid pattern recognition, mental math fluency, and systematic problem-solving approaches.

H3: Mathematics Reasoning (No Changes)

**Format:**
- 60 questions, 30 minutes (unchanged)
- No calculator permitted (unchanged)
- Content areas: algebra, geometry, fractions/decimals/percentages, ratios, data interpretation (unchanged)

**Content:**
Mathematics content continues to align with Victorian Curriculum through Year 8/9. Question difficulty and topic distribution remain consistent with previous years.

**Preparation Impact:**
Curriculum-based mathematics preparation remains essential. Focus continues on rapid problem-solving, mental calculation fluency, and applying concepts efficiently.

H3: Reading Reasoning (Minor Platform Refinements)

**Format:**
- 50 questions, 35 minutes (unchanged)
- Hybrid format: standalone questions + passage-based questions (unchanged)
- Content mix: grammar, vocabulary, sentence structure, passage comprehension (unchanged)

**Platform Updates:**
Minor improvements to text presentation and highlighting tools make passage navigation slightly easier. Question functionality remains unchanged.

**Preparation Impact:**
Minimal impact. Continue practicing both standalone language questions and passage-based comprehension with emphasis on time management across the hybrid format.

H3: Writing (Enhanced Rubric Clarity)

**Format:**
- 40 minutes (unchanged)
- 2 prompts provided, student chooses 1 (unchanged)
- Creative OR Persuasive writing (unchanged)

**Rubric Updates:**
VCAA has published more detailed rubric descriptors for 2026, providing clearer guidance on what constitutes superior ability in writing. The actual scoring criteria remain unchanged, but explanations are more transparent.

**Preparation Impact:**
Minor positive impact. The enhanced rubric clarity helps students understand exactly what markers assess, allowing more targeted writing practice.

H2: Application Process Changes

Understanding application updates ensures you don't miss important deadlines or requirements.

H3: Application Timeline (No Changes)

**Key Dates Remain Consistent:**
- Applications open: Early April 2025
- Applications close: Late May 2025
- Test date: Mid-June 2025
- Results released: Mid-August 2025
- Offers announced: Late August 2025

**What This Means:**
Families can plan preparation timelines using previous years' schedules as guides. The 4-month window from applications opening to test date remains standard.

H3: Eligibility Requirements (No Changes)

**Requirements Remain:**
- Current Year 8 students applying for Year 9 entry
- Australian citizenship or permanent residency
- Residence within reasonable commuting distance of preferred schools
- Payment of application fee (currently $50)

**What This Means:**
Eligibility criteria remain consistent. Families should verify residency requirements for their preferred schools using VCAA guidelines.

H3: School Selection Process (No Changes)

**Selection Process:**
Students continue to rank school preferences on their application. Offers are allocated based on:
- Achieving superior ability in at least 3 of 5 sections
- Placement score calculation combining test results and school assessment
- Student preferences and available places

**What This Means:**
Strategic school ranking remains important. Research each school thoroughly and rank genuinely preferred schools first.


H2: Scoring and Placement Changes

Understanding scoring updates helps set realistic goals and interpret results accurately.

H3: Superior Ability Thresholds (Methodology Unchanged)

**How It Works:**
Students must demonstrate superior ability in at least 3 of the 5 test sections to be eligible for offers. Superior ability thresholds are determined using statistical analysis of the cohort's performance each year.

**2026 Clarification:**
While the methodology remains unchanged, VCAA has provided clearer documentation explaining:
- How raw scores are converted to scaled scores
- How superior ability thresholds are determined for each section
- How the 3-of-5 requirement works in practice

**What This Means:**
No change to difficulty or competitiveness. The enhanced transparency simply helps families understand the process better.

H3: Placement Score Calculation (Weightings Unchanged)

**Formula Remains:**
- Selective Entry test results: Majority weighting
- Primary school assessment: Minority weighting (approximately 20-25%)

**What This Means:**
Test performance remains the dominant factor in placement score calculation. Strong school results help but cannot compensate for insufficient test performance.


H2: What Hasn't Changed (and Why That Matters)

Understanding stability in the test structure helps maintain confidence in preparation approaches.

H3: Core Test Philosophy

**Cognitive Assessment Focus:**
The test continues to prioritize cognitive abilities and reasoning skills over rote memorization. This philosophy underpins all question types and ensures students are assessed on thinking abilities rather than taught content alone.

**Why This Matters:**
Preparation must emphasize reasoning development, pattern recognition, and logical thinking—not just memorization. This approach remains valid for 2026.

H3: Time Pressure and Question Volume

**Demanding Pace Continues:**
The test maintains its characteristic time pressure:
- 30 seconds per question for Verbal and Mathematics sections
- 36 seconds per question for Quantitative section
- 42 seconds per question for Reading section

**Why This Matters:**
Speed and accuracy remain equally important. Practice under timed conditions continues to be essential preparation.

H3: Competitive Landscape

**Approximately 32-35% Offer Rate:**
Roughly one-third of test-takers receive offers to selective entry schools. This success rate has remained relatively stable over recent years.

**Why This Matters:**
The test remains highly competitive. Strong preparation across all five sections is essential, not optional.


H2: How These Changes Impact Your Preparation

Translating change analysis into actionable preparation adjustments.

H3: Study Materials and Resources

**What to Use:**
- Official VCAA 2026 practice materials (most current)
- Preparation resources from 2024-2025 (remain highly relevant)
- Section-specific practice focusing on speed and accuracy

**What to Avoid:**
- Materials older than 2022 (may not reflect current question styles)

H3: Preparation Timeline

**Recommended Timeline:**
The lack of major changes means established preparation timelines remain effective:
- **6 months before test:** Begin skill development across all sections
- **4 months before test:** Intensify practice with timed conditions
- **2 months before test:** Focus on weak sections and full practice tests
- **Final month:** Targeted practice, test simulations, confidence building

**Why This Works:**
Proven preparation timelines remain valid because test structure and difficulty are consistent.

H3: Section Priority

**Balanced Approach Remains Essential:**
With the 3-of-5 superior ability requirement unchanged, students need strong performance across multiple sections, not just one or two strengths.

**Strategic Focus:**
- Identify your 3-4 strongest sections and aim for superior ability in those
- Bring weaker sections up to competitive levels
- Don't neglect writing—it's often the differentiator between offers


H2: Common Misconceptions About 2026 Changes

Clarifying misinformation helps families prepare accurately.

H3: Myth: "The Test Is Getting Easier"

**Reality:**
There's no evidence the 2026 test is easier than previous years. Superior ability thresholds adjust based on cohort performance, maintaining consistent competitiveness regardless of absolute difficulty.

H3: Myth: "New School Places Reduce Competition"

**Reality:**
The four existing selective entry schools continue with similar place allocations. While individual school capacities may fluctuate slightly, overall competition remains high.

H3: Myth: "Writing Counts Less Now"

**Reality:**
Writing remains one of the five sections required for superior ability determination. Strong writing performance is just as valuable as strong performance in other sections.
