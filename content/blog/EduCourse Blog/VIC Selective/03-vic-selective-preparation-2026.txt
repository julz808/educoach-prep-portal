ARTICLE TITLE:
VIC Selective Entry Preparation 2026: Complete 16-Week Study Plan

INTRODUCTION:

Victoria's Selective Entry test determines placement at four of Melbourne's most prestigious public secondary schools: Melbourne High, Mac.Robertson Girls' High, Nossal High, and Suzanne Cory High. With approximately 5,000+ students competing for roughly 1,600 Year 9 places, strategic preparation isn't just helpful—it's essential.

The test comprises five demanding sections completed in under 3 hours: General Ability Verbal (60 Q, 30 min), General Ability Quantitative (50 Q, 30 min), Mathematics Reasoning (60 Q, 30 min), Reading Reasoning (50 Q, 35 min), and Writing (40 min). Success requires achieving "superior ability" in at least 3 of these 5 sections while competing against thousands of academically strong peers.

This comprehensive 16-week preparation plan provides a systematic, week-by-week approach to mastering all five sections, building speed and accuracy, developing test-taking strategies, and peaking for test day in mid-June 2025.

H2: Understanding the Preparation Timeline

Strategic timing maximizes skill development while avoiding burnout.

H3: Why 16 Weeks?

**Optimal Skill Development Window:**
Sixteen weeks (approximately 4 months) provides sufficient time to:
- Assess current performance across all sections
- Identify and address specific weaknesses
- Build cognitive skills that develop gradually (reasoning, pattern recognition)
- Practice extensively under timed conditions
- Complete multiple full-length practice tests
- Fine-tune strategies and peak for test day

**Not Too Early, Not Too Late:**
- Starting earlier risks burnout and forgetting skills
- Starting later leaves insufficient time for comprehensive preparation
- Four months balances thoroughness with maintaining motivation

H3: When to Start for June 2025 Test

**Recommended Start Date: Early-to-Mid February 2025**

**Timeline:**
- Week 1 (Feb 10-16): Diagnostic assessment and baseline
- Weeks 2-13 (Feb 17 - May 11): Intensive skill development and practice
- Weeks 14-15 (May 12-25): Final preparation and full practice tests
- Week 16 (May 26 - June 1): Light review, rest, confidence building
- Test Week (June 2-8): Test day!

**Adjust Based on Your Situation:**
- If starting earlier: Extend weeks 2-13 phase with more gradual progression
- If starting later: Intensify daily practice hours (but avoid cramming)


H2: The 16-Week Preparation Framework

Understanding the overall structure helps implement the plan effectively.

H3: Phase 1: Assessment & Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

**Goals:**
- Establish performance baseline across all five sections
- Identify specific strengths and weaknesses
- Build fundamental skills in weak areas
- Introduce timed practice gradually

**Time Commitment:**
6-8 hours per week (approximately 1 hour daily Monday-Friday, 2 hours Saturday, Sunday rest)

H3: Phase 2: Skill Building & Section Mastery (Weeks 5-10)

**Goals:**
- Intensive practice in each section
- Develop speed alongside accuracy
- Learn section-specific strategies
- Build stamina for sustained concentration

**Time Commitment:**
10-12 hours per week (approximately 1.5 hours daily Monday-Friday, 3 hours Saturday, Sunday rest)

H3: Phase 3: Integration & Full Tests (Weeks 11-13)

**Goals:**
- Complete multiple full-length practice tests under test conditions
- Refine time management across all sections
- Address remaining weak points
- Build test-taking confidence

**Time Commitment:**
10-12 hours per week (including 2.5-hour practice tests)

H3: Phase 4: Peak & Taper (Weeks 14-16)

**Goals:**
- Fine-tune strategies based on practice test results
- Light review of challenging concepts
- Rest and mental preparation
- Maintain confidence without overworking

**Time Commitment:**
6-8 hours per week (tapering to 2-3 hours in final week)

H2: Week-by-Week Breakdown: Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4)

Detailed guidance for the assessment and foundation phase.

H3: Week 1: Diagnostic Assessment

**Monday-Wednesday (3 hours total):**
- Complete full diagnostic test under timed conditions (2.5 hours)
- Review results carefully, noting strengths and weaknesses (30 min)

**Thursday-Friday (2 hours total):**
- Analyze sub-skill performance in each section
- Identify 2-3 priority improvement areas
- Create personalized focus list

**Saturday (2 hours):**
- Research test format and requirements thoroughly
- Watch tutorial videos on test-taking strategies
- Set specific performance goals for each section

**Sunday:**
Rest day

**Week 1 Goals:**
✓ Baseline scores established
✓ Clear understanding of test structure
✓ Identified personal strengths and weaknesses
✓ Created targeted improvement plan

H3: Week 2-4: Foundation Building

**Monday (1.5 hours each week):**
- General Ability Verbal: 30 min vocabulary practice + 30 min analogies
- Mathematics Reasoning: 30 min review Year 8 concepts

**Tuesday (1.5 hours):**
- General Ability Quantitative: 45 min number series practice
- Reading Reasoning: 45 min grammar and vocabulary questions

**Wednesday (1.5 hours):**
- Mathematics Reasoning: 45 min problem-solving practice
- General Ability Verbal: 45 min code breaking and letter patterns

**Thursday (1.5 hours):**
- Reading Reasoning: 45 min passage comprehension practice
- General Ability Quantitative: 45 min number grids and matrices

**Friday (1.5 hours):**
- Writing: 45 min persuasive writing practice (Week 2), creative writing (Week 3), alternate (Week 4)
- Review week's progress: 45 min

**Saturday (2-3 hours):**
- Timed section practice (rotate sections each week)
- Review incorrect answers and understand mistakes
- Complete 20-30 questions in weakest section

**Sunday:**
Rest day (light reading for vocabulary development optional)


H2: Week-by-Week Breakdown: Phase 2 (Weeks 5-10)

Intensive skill development and section mastery.

H3: Week 5-6: Speed Development

**Focus:** Building speed while maintaining accuracy

**Daily Structure (Monday-Friday, 1.5-2 hours each):**
- 30 min: Timed drill in rotating section (aim for 90% accuracy at faster pace)
- 30 min: Untimed practice in challenging sub-skills
- 30 min: Review and error analysis
- 30 min: Vocabulary building and mental math drills

**Saturday (3 hours):**
- Complete two full sections under timed conditions
- Thorough review of all incorrect answers
- Identify patterns in mistakes

**Sunday:**
Rest with light reading

**Week 5-6 Goals:**
✓ Improved speed in all sections (aiming for 80-85% completion)
✓ Maintaining 85%+ accuracy
✓ Reduced careless errors

H3: Week 7-8: Section-Specific Strategies

**Focus:** Mastering unique strategies for each section

**Section Rotation Schedule:**
- Week 7 Monday-Tuesday: General Ability Verbal intensive
- Week 7 Wednesday-Thursday: Mathematics Reasoning intensive
- Week 7 Friday-Saturday: Writing intensive
- Week 8 Monday-Tuesday: General Ability Quantitative intensive
- Week 8 Wednesday-Thursday: Reading Reasoning intensive
- Week 8 Friday-Saturday: Full practice test

**Intensive Section Days (2 hours):**
- 45 min: Strategy instruction and examples
- 60 min: Timed practice applying strategies
- 15 min: Reflection and strategy refinement

**Week 7-8 Goals:**
✓ Mastered elimination techniques for each section
✓ Developed personal time management approach
✓ Identified question types to attempt vs. skip

H3: Week 9-10: Stamina and Consistency

**Focus:** Building endurance for full test experience

**Monday-Friday (2 hours each):**
- 45 min: Timed practice in rotation (all five sections covered weekly)
- 45 min: Practice in weakest area
- 30 min: Writing practice (building to full 40-min responses)

**Saturday (3-4 hours):**
- Week 9: Complete three consecutive sections under test conditions (simulate half the test)
- Week 10: Complete full practice test #1 under exact test conditions

**Sunday:**
Rest day (Week 10: light review of practice test errors)

**Week 9-10 Goals:**
✓ Completed first full practice test
✓ Sustained concentration for 2.5+ hours
✓ Identified time management issues to address


H2: Week-by-Week Breakdown: Phase 3 (Weeks 11-13)

Integration and full test practice.

H3: Week 11: Practice Test #2 and Analysis

**Monday-Thursday (1.5 hours each):**
- Targeted practice addressing weaknesses revealed in Practice Test #1
- Focus on speed in sections where time was insufficient
- Practice question types with high error rates

**Friday (2 hours):**
- Review test-taking strategies
- Mental preparation techniques
- Plan for Saturday's practice test

**Saturday (3 hours):**
- Complete Practice Test #2 under exact test conditions
- Time each section precisely
- Simulate test-day procedures (breaks, materials, etc.)

**Sunday (2 hours):**
- Detailed analysis of Practice Test #2
- Compare scores to Practice Test #1
- Update focus areas list

**Week 11 Goals:**
✓ Second complete practice test completed
✓ Measurable improvement from Test #1
✓ Clear picture of remaining gaps

H3: Week 12: Intensive Weak-Point Work

**Monday-Friday (2 hours each):**
- Deep dive into remaining problem areas
- Targeted drills in lowest-performing sub-skills
- Strategy refinement for challenging question types

**Saturday (2 hours):**
- Mixed section practice with focus on weak areas
- Timed practice building confidence

**Sunday:**
Rest with light mental math and vocabulary review

**Week 12 Goals:**
✓ Significant improvement in identified weak areas
✓ Confidence in approaching previously difficult questions
✓ Ready for final practice test

H3: Week 13: Practice Test #3 and Final Adjustments

**Monday-Wednesday (1.5 hours each):**
- Light practice maintaining skills
- Review notes from previous practice tests
- Writing practice (one full essay)

**Thursday (2 hours):**
- Review all test-taking strategies
- Mental preparation and visualization
- Rest and prepare for Practice Test #3

**Friday:**
Light review only (30 min)

**Saturday (3 hours):**
- Complete Practice Test #3 under exact test conditions
- This is your dress rehearsal

**Sunday (2 hours):**
- Final analysis of Practice Test #3
- Celebrate improvements
- Note any last minor adjustments needed

**Week 13 Goals:**
✓ Third practice test completed with strong performance
✓ Consistent scores across multiple practice tests
✓ Confidence in all sections


H2: Week-by-Week Breakdown: Phase 4 (Weeks 14-16)

Peak performance and taper.

H3: Week 14-15: Final Polish

**Daily Practice (1 hour each day):**
- Light review of challenging concepts
- Maintain skills without overworking
- Focus on confidence-building

**Weekend Activities:**
- Saturday Week 14: Mixed section practice (1.5 hours)
- Sunday Week 14: Rest
- Saturday Week 15: Light timed practice (1 hour)
- Sunday Week 15: Complete rest

**Week 14-15 Goals:**
✓ Skills maintained without burnout
✓ Mental freshness preserved
✓ Confidence high

H3: Week 16: Test Week

**Monday-Wednesday (30 min each day):**
- Very light review only
- Mental math drills (keep mind sharp)
- Vocabulary review
- **No intensive practice**

**Thursday-Friday:**
- Complete rest from academic work
- Light physical activity
- Early bedtimes
- Positive visualization

**Saturday/Sunday (Test Day):**
- Good breakfast
- Arrive early at test center
- Implement all practiced strategies
- Trust your preparation

**Week 16 Goals:**
✓ Rested and mentally fresh
✓ Confident and ready
✓ Peak performance on test day


H2: Section-Specific Preparation Strategies

Tailored approaches for each section within the 16-week plan.

H3: General Ability - Verbal (60 Q, 30 min)

**Ongoing Throughout 16 Weeks:**
- Daily vocabulary: 10 new words per day (770+ words total)
- Weekly focus: Analogies (Weeks 1-3), Letter patterns (Weeks 4-6), Code breaking (Weeks 7-9), Mixed practice (Weeks 10-16)
- Speed target: Complete 60 questions in 30 minutes by Week 10
- Accuracy target: 85%+ by Week 13

**Key Strategies:**
- Build systematic analogy-solving approach
- Learn common letter pattern types
- Practice code-breaking under time pressure
- Develop vocabulary through context, not flashcards alone

H3: General Ability - Quantitative (50 Q, 30 min)

**Ongoing Throughout 16 Weeks:**
- Weekly focus rotates: Number series (Weeks 1-4), Number grids (Weeks 5-8), Word problems (Weeks 9-12), Mixed (Weeks 13-16)
- Mental math: Daily 10-minute drills
- Speed target: Complete 50 questions in 30 minutes by Week 11
- Accuracy target: 85%+ by Week 14

**Key Strategies:**
- Master systematic pattern-finding approaches
- Practice grid navigation strategies
- Develop rapid calculation skills
- Learn to skip unsolvable questions quickly

H3: Mathematics Reasoning (60 Q, 30 min)

**Ongoing Throughout 16 Weeks:**
- Content review: Algebra (Weeks 1-2), Geometry (Weeks 3-4), Fractions/Decimals (Weeks 5-6), Ratios (Weeks 7-8), Data (Weeks 9-10), Mixed (Weeks 11-16)
- Mental math: Daily 15-minute drills
- Speed target: Complete 60 questions in 30 minutes by Week 12
- Accuracy target: 90%+ by Week 15

**Key Strategies:**
- Master Year 8 curriculum thoroughly
- Practice speed calculations without calculator
- Learn multi-step problem-solving approaches
- Memorize key formulas and relationships

H3: Reading Reasoning (50 Q, 35 min)

**Ongoing Throughout 16 Weeks:**
- Grammar focus (Weeks 1-4), Vocabulary (Weeks 5-8), Passages (Weeks 9-12), Mixed (Weeks 13-16)
- Daily reading: 20 minutes of complex texts
- Speed target: Complete 50 questions in 35 minutes by Week 10
- Accuracy target: 85%+ by Week 13

**Key Strategies:**
- Master standalone question types first
- Build efficient passage reading strategies
- Practice time allocation between standalones and passages
- Learn grammar rules systematically

H3: Writing (40 min, choose 1 of 2 prompts)

**Ongoing Throughout 16 Weeks:**
- Week 1-3: Essay structure and planning (5-min plans)
- Week 4-7: Persuasive writing development
- Week 8-11: Creative writing development
- Week 12-14: Timed full essays alternating types
- Week 15-16: Polish and confidence

**Practice Schedule:**
- Weeks 1-6: Plan only (5 min planning practice)
- Weeks 7-10: Plan + partial essays (20-25 min writing)
- Weeks 11-16: Full timed essays (40 min total)
- Minimum: 12-15 complete essays by test day

**Key Strategies:**
- Develop fast planning technique (3-4 min maximum)
- Build vocabulary for both creative and persuasive writing
- Practice both prompt types equally
- Learn to write clearly under time pressure


H2: Weekly Study Schedule Template

Practical day-by-day structure you can adapt.

H3: Monday through Friday Pattern

**3:30-4:00pm:** Mental break, snack, settle in
**4:00-4:45pm:** Timed section practice (rotating daily)
**4:45-5:00pm:** Short break
**5:00-5:45pm:** Targeted weak area practice
**5:45-6:00pm:** Review and error analysis
**Evening:** Light vocabulary or mental math (10-15 min)

**Adjust based on your schedule:** Morning study, weekend-heavy, etc.

H3: Saturday Intensive Structure

**9:00-10:30am:** Full timed section practice (or practice test)
**10:30-11:00am:** Break
**11:00-12:00pm:** Review and error analysis
**12:00pm:** Done for the day!

**Keep Saturdays structured but not overwhelming.**

H3: Sunday Rest Principle

**Why Rest Matters:**
- Allows information consolidation
- Prevents burnout
- Maintains motivation
- Supports long-term performance

**Optional Light Activity:**
- Pleasure reading (vocabulary building)
- Light mental math games
- Educational videos
- **Nothing stressful or timed**


H2: Monitoring Progress and Adjusting

Systematic tracking ensures the plan stays effective.

H3: Weekly Check-Ins

**Every Friday Evening:**
- Review the week's practice performance
- Calculate average accuracy by section
- Note improvement areas
- Adjust next week's focus if needed

**Track These Metrics:**
- Questions completed vs. time spent (speed improvement)
- Accuracy percentage by section
- Question types with high error rates
- Sections feeling more/less confident

H3: Practice Test Milestones

**After Each Practice Test:**
- Calculate scaled scores if possible
- Compare to previous practice test
- Celebrate improvements
- Identify 2-3 specific areas needing focus
- Adjust remaining weeks' emphasis

**Expect:**
- Practice Test #1 (Week 10): Baseline competitive performance
- Practice Test #2 (Week 11): 5-10% improvement
- Practice Test #3 (Week 13): Another 3-5% improvement
- Test Day: Peak performance


H2: Balancing School, Life, and Preparation

Realistic integration prevents burnout.

H3: Managing School Commitments

**Priorities:**
1. Maintain strong Year 8 grades (counts toward placement score)
2. VIC Selective preparation
3. Reasonable rest and recreation

**Integration Strategies:**
- Use school mathematics homework as practice (but add timed component)
- Leverage English assignments for writing skill development
- Discuss with teachers if workload becomes unmanageable

H3: Maintaining Balance

**Non-Negotiables:**
- One full rest day per week (usually Sunday)
- 8-9 hours sleep nightly
- Physical activity 3-4 times per week
- Time with friends/family

**Warning Signs of Overwork:**
- Declining school grades
- Persistent fatigue
- Loss of motivation
- Increased errors despite practice

**If These Occur:**
- Reduce daily study hours by 25%
- Add extra rest days
- Consult with parents about adjustment
